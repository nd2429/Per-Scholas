Stories

Now that you know a little about me. Let's dig in a little deeper about myself, hobbies and interests. In the past, my career was in the field of Psychology and I worked with people with developmental disabilities. Although I cared deeply about every case I had and knew I was making a difference, most of the time we were limited with what we were able to do for them. I went to school for Psychology and Counseling because I wanted to help people and make a difference in my idividudals' lives and in the field. Towards the end of finishing my second degree, I came to the realization that I cannot do what I went in with the intention of doing because of many factors that influence the field. This left me feeling upset and defeated. the epiphany then came to me. I wanted to be more independent and creative in my life and in my career.



I wanted to expand my creativity that I was suppressing and while curiously doing research about my likes and dislikes, I came across coding and all the possibilities that come along with it. Therefore, I started to teach myself on my free time for fun. It was easy at fist until it wasn't. However, I love a challenge. During the lockdown for COVID-19 I found myself wanting to make a change in my life and pursue my purpose and find out what hobbies I would even like, since I never got a chance to explore them fully growing up.

Gardening, health and Mental Health

I didn't just stop there. I began doing research about my interest and hobbies. I realized that health is very important to me and I decided to come up with a plan to eat healty and get in shape. Not only for the aesthetics but also, becuase of the food that is produced for us is not very nutritious and somewhat questionable. However, I took it one step further. I decided I wanted to grow my own food to the highest of my ability and limitations. This is my first time gardening/growing, so I'm startimg slow. I really enjoy gardening now and find it therapeutic.

Health is not just about eating and working out. Mental health is also very important and is what closes the holistic approach in every aspect of what is to be considered healthy. Your mental health can affect your physical health and vice versa. Stress, burn out, anxiety, depression and other factors can very much affect you mentally and physically. These symptoms are prevalent in almost any industry you work at or even at home. this can affect your productivity, health, emotions, amongst other things in every aspect of your life. This is why it is very important to stop and check in with yourself once in a while and assess how you're feeling phisically and mentally; along with being aware of what you're eating. Food also has an impact on physical and mental health.
